Introduction

Phenotypic heterogeneity in cell populations has been shown to play critical roles in determining biologically and clinically significant phenotypes. The goal of this project is to identify robustness QTLs, loci in the human genome that are associated with within-individual variation in gene expression levels.

Data collection

We collected single cell RNA-seq data from YRI invididuals using the C1 platform. We pooled and sequenced each C1 chip on an Illumina HiSeq 2500.

We identified sample swaps and matched samples to individuals using verifyBamID.

Next, we filtered low quality samples and both over- and lowly-expressed genes for downstream analysis.

We then performed dimensionality reduction on the quality-controlled samples and genes to identify the remaining major sources of variation.

QTL mapping

We first investigated the concordance between scRNA-Seq and matched bulk RNA-seq data.

We then built an interactive browser to view the distribution of scRNA-Seq counts stratified by genotype at top eQTLs in the matched bulk RNA-Seq data.

Next, we estimated means and dispersions per individual per gene. We then treated these as continuous phenotypes and mapped QTLs using QTLtools.
